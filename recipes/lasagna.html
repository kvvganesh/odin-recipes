Lasagna

Lasagna, also known by the plural form lasagne, is a type of pasta made in wide, flat sheets. It is also a dish originating in Italian cuisine, where it is served in a number of ways, including in broth (lasagne in brodo), but is best known for its use in a baked dish made by stacking layers of pasta, alternating with fillings such as ragù (ground meats and tomato sauce), béchamel sauce, vegetables, cheeses (which may include ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan), and seasonings and spices. Typically, cooked pasta is assembled with the other ingredients, topped with grated cheese, and then baked in an oven (al forno): regional variations of this dish are found across Italy.

Ingredients:

    Pasta:

  • Lasagna Noodles: 1 box (12–16 oz / 350g–450g), either traditional (boil-first) or oven-ready

    • Meat and Sauce:

  • Meat & SauceGround Meat: 1–1.5 lbs (usually a mix of 1/2 lb lean ground beef and 1/2 lb ground Italian sausage for the best flavor)
  • Aromatics: 1 medium yellow onion (diced) and 2–4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • Tomato Base: 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce or a combination of 1 can crushed tomatoes (28 oz) and 1 can tomato sauce (8 oz)
  • Tomato Paste: 2 tablespoons (optional, to thicken the sauce)
  • Seasoning & Sugar: 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning,1/2 teaspoon dried basil, salt and black pepper to taste, and 1 pinch of sugar to balance the tomato acidity

    • Cheese Layer:

  • Ricotta or Cottage Cheese: 1.5–2 lbs (whole-milk ricotta is traditional, but cottage cheese is a popular and creamy alternative)
  • Mozzarella: 3 cups (12 oz) shredded, low-moisture
  • Parmesan: 1/3 to 3/4 cup freshly grated
  • Egg: 1 large egg (beaten, acts as a binder for the ricotta)
  • Fresh Herbs: 1/4 cup fresh parsley or basil (chopped, for stirring into the cheese)

Steps to Prepare:

    1. Prep the Meat Sauce

  1. Brown the meat:Cook the ground beef and Italian sausage in a large skillet over medium-high heat until no pink remains.
  2. Drain grease: Spoon out and discard excess fat from the pan.
  3. Sauté aromatics: Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the meat, cooking for 3–4 minutes until soft.
  4. Simmer sauce: Pour in the crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, Italian seasoning, dried basil, sugar, salt, and pepper.Reduce heat: Cover and simmer on low for 15–20 minutes to blend flavors.

    5. 2. Mix the Cheese Filling

  5. Combine ingredients: Stir together the ricotta cheese, beaten egg, grated parmesan, and chopped fresh herbs in a medium bowl.
  6. Season: Add a pinch of salt and black pepper to taste.
  7. Shred mozzarella: Keep your shredded mozzarella in a separate bowl for easy sprinkling during assembly.

    8. 3. Handle the Noodles

  8. Boil method: If using traditional noodles, boil them in salted water according to package instructions, drain, and lay flat on foil to prevent sticking.
  9. Oven-ready method: Skip boiling completely; the moisture from the sauce cooks them in the oven.

    10. 4. Assemble the Layers

  10. Base: Spread 1 cup of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  11. Layer 1: Place a flat layer of noodles over the sauce.
  12. Layer 2: Spread a third of the ricotta mixture over the noodles.
  13. Layer 3: Sprinkle with one cup of mozzarella cheese.
  14. Layer 4: Top with 1 cup of meat sauce.
  15. Repeat: Repeat the noodle-ricotta-mozzarella-sauce pattern two more times.
  16. Top: Finish with a final layer of noodles, the remaining sauce, and the rest of the mozzarella and parmesan.

    17. 5. Bake and Rest

  17. Cover: Tent the dish with aluminum foil, ensuring it does not touch the top cheese layer.
  18. Bake warm: Bake at 190°C (375°F) for 25 minutes with the foil on.Bake crisp: Remove the foil and bake for another 20 minutes until the cheese bubbles and turns golden brown.
  19. Rest: Cool for 15 minutes before slicing so the layers hold their shape.
