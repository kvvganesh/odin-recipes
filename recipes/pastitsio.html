Pastitsio

Pastitsio is a traditional Greek baked pasta dish often called “Greek lasagna” because it has layers of pasta, meat sauce, and a creamy topping. The name comes from the Italian word “pasticcio,” meaning a mixed or baked dish. It usually contains: A layer of tubular pasta (like bucatini or penne) A spiced minced meat sauce A thick béchamel (white sauce) layer on top Cheese baked until golden and delicious It is a popular comfort food in Greece and is commonly served at family gatherings and celebrations.

Ingredients

    For the pasta layer:

  • 400 g pasta (bucatini, penne, or similar)
  • tablespoons butter
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt

    • For the meat sauce:

  • 500 g minced beef (or lamb)
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 2–3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 2 cups tomato sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper
  • Olive oil

    • For béchamel sauce:

  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 4 tablespoons flour
  • 3 cups milk
  • 1 egg yolk
  • ½ cup grated cheese
  • Salt and nutmeg (optional)

Procedure

    Cook the pasta

  1. Boil pasta in salted water until slightly firm.
  2. Drain and mix with butter and cheese.
  3. Set aside.

    4. Make the meat sauce

  4. Heat olive oil and sauté onions and garlic.
  5. Add minced meat and cook until browned.
  6. Add tomato sauce, tomato paste, spices, salt, and pepper.
  7. Simmer for 15–20 minutes.

    8. Prepare the béchamel

  8. Melt butter in a pan.
  9. Add flour and cook for a minute.
  10. Slowly add milk while stirring.
  11. Cook until thick.
  12. Add egg yolk, cheese, and seasoning.

    13. Layer the pastitsio

  13. Put half the pasta in a baking dish.
  14. Add the meat sauce.
  15. Add remaining pasta.
  16. Spread béchamel sauce on top.
  17. Sprinkle cheese.

    18. Bake

  18. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for about 40–45 minutes.
  19. Bake until the top becomes golden.
