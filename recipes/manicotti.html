Manicotti

Manicotti is a traditional Italian-American pasta dish made with large tube-shaped pasta shells that are stuffed with a creamy filling, covered in sauce, and baked until hot and cheesy. The name “manicotti” comes from the Italian word “manicotto,” meaning “sleeve” or “little sleeve,” referring to the pasta’s tube shape. It is usually prepared with: Large pasta tubes (manicotti shells) Ricotta cheese as the main filling Mozzarella and Parmesan cheese Tomato-based marinara sauce Herbs like parsley, basil, and Italian seasoning There are many variations: Cheese manicotti – filled mainly with ricotta and other cheeses Spinach manicotti – includes spinach in the filling Meat manicotti – includes beef, sausage, or other meats Manicotti is often served as a comfort food and is popular for family dinners, holidays, and special occasions because it can be prepared ahead and baked when needed. It is similar to Cannelloni, another stuffed pasta dish, though manicotti shells are typically larger and have a ridged texture.

Ingredients:

    For the manicotti:

  • 12 manicotti pasta shells (or large pasta tubes)
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley (or 1 teaspoon dried parsley)
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)

    • For the sauce:

  • 2–3 cups marinara/tomato sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Procedure:

    Cook the pasta

    1. Boil water with salt.
    2. Cook manicotti shells until slightly soft but still firm (al dente).
    3. Drain and let them cool so they are easy to handle.
    4. Prepare the filling:

      5. In a bowl, mix:

      • ricotta cheese
      • mozzarella
      • Parmesan
      • egg
      • parsley
      • Italian seasoning
      • salt and pepper
    5. Mix until well combined.

    Prepare the sauce

    1. Heat olive oil in a pan.
    2. Add garlic and sauté briefly.
    3. Add marinara sauce and simmer for a few minutes.

      4. Fill the shells

    4. Carefully stuff each manicotti shell with the cheese mixture.
    5. Use a spoon or piping bag for easier filling.

    Assemble

    1. Spread some sauce in a baking dish.
    2. Place stuffed manicotti shells on top.
    3. Cover with remaining sauce.
    4. Sprinkle extra mozzarella and Parmesan.

    Bake

    1. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for about 25–30 minutes.
    2. Bake until the cheese melts and the sauce is bubbling
